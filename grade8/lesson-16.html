Key Concepts

  • A rotation turns a figure around a center point.
  • Rotations preserve lengths and angle measures.
  • About the origin: 90° CCW: (x,y)→(−y,x), 90° CW: (x,y)→(y,−x), 180°: (x,y)→(−x,−y).
  • About center (a,b): shift to origin, rotate, shift back.
90°CCW: (x,y)→(−y,x) 90°CW: (x,y)→(y,−x) 180°: (x,y)→(−x,−y)

Worked Example

Example: Rotate A(3,2) 90° CCW about origin
Apply rule (x,y)→(−y,x) → A′(−2,3)
