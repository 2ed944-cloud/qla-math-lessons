Learning Objectives & Keywords

Learning Objectives

  • Interpret \(p+q\) on a number line as moving \(q\) units from \(p\): right if \(q>0\), left if \(q<0\).
  • Understand subtraction as adding the additive inverse: \(p-q = p+(-q)\).
  • Add/subtract rational numbers (integers, fractions, decimals) and justify with visuals and arithmetic.
  • Find least common multiples using prime factorization.

Prime Numbers

A prime is an integer \( \ge 2 \) with exactly two positive factors: 1 and itself. A composite has more than two. 1 is neither.

Prime Factorization (Exponent Form)

Write each number as a product of primes using exponents, e.g., \( 60 = 2^{2}\cdot 3^{1}\cdot 5^{1} \).
